# Sahamigo Visual Reference — Direction B: "Teman Belajar yang Santai"

Curated from publicly observable patterns of two real products (via getdesign.md research), adapted — not cloned — for Sahamigo's brand direction. Treat this as a starting palette/type system to react to, not a final spec.

## Why these two

- **Starbucks** — calm, warm earth-tone system built around a cream canvas. Borrowed for: the *structure* of a calm neutral base + a muted accent tier (not the literal green hue).
- **Notion** — warm minimalism, serif headings, soft surfaces. Borrowed for: typography warmth and uncluttered layout density, which fits a "learning companion" tone better than a sterile dashboard.

## Color direction

Adapt Starbucks' tiered-accent structure, re-hued toward Sahamigo's chosen **sage-teal** instead of brand green:

| Token | Role | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Canvas | Background | Warm cream/off-white, not stark white |
| Accent 1 (darkest) | Primary CTA, active nav | Deep sage-teal |
| Accent 2 | Secondary actions, links | Mid sage-teal |
| Accent 3 | Hover/highlight states | Light sage-teal tint |
| Accent 4 (lightest) | Subtle fills, badges (e.g. "EOD" badge) | Near-cream sage tint |
| Text primary | Body copy | Warm dark brown/charcoal, not pure black |

Avoid: stark white canvas, pure black text, neon/saturated greens — these read as generic SaaS, not "calm companion."

## Typography direction

Notion-inspired warmth, not Notion's literal stack:

- Headings: a humanist serif or serif-leaning display face — gives "teacher/companion" warmth over "fintech terminal" coldness.
- Body: a clean humanist sans for data-heavy areas (tables, ratios, chat text) — readability over personality.
- Avoid geometric/grotesk sans for headings — that's the "generic AI slop" look Markus explicitly wants to avoid.

## Layout/component cues

- Soft, rounded surfaces for cards (stock detail, chat bubbles) — no hard edges or harsh shadows.
- Starbucks' pill-shaped CTA pattern works well for the "Tanya AI soal saham ini" action — friendly, inviting, not transactional-looking like a "Beli" button.
- Generous whitespace and low visual density per Notion's minimalism — important for EOD data tables so they don't feel like a trading terminal.

## Explicit boundary

This is inspiration for tone and structure only. Do not reuse exact hex values, exact type families, or any trademarked visual assets from Starbucks or Notion. Sally/Caravaggio define Sahamigo's actual tokens from this direction.
